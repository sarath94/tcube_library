EVEN EXIST! SO THIS FIRST PART OF MY NEW SERIES, I HAVE LOOKED AROUND AND FOUND
299 OF THE MOST COMMON NAMES OF COMPOUNDS AND MADE A LIST OF ALL OF THEM.
IN THE LIST, IT ALSO CONTAINS THEIR CHEMICAL NAME AND FORMULA. SINCE I'M USING
AN APPLE II+, THE FORMULA IS GOING TO BE AS FOLLOWS.
   1) SINCE THERE IS NO LOWER CASE, ALL LOWER CASE LETTERS UNDER THE FORMULA  
       COLUMN WILL BE IN BRACKES. ([]).
      EXAMPLE... TIN.. S[N] AND SODIUM CHLORIDE... N[A]C[L]
 
   2) ALL PERIODS, '.', IN THE FORMULA COLUMN MEANS X AMOUNT OF POLYATOMIC IONS
      EXAMPLE... N[I]SO4.7H2O MEANS..  THERE IS ONE MOLECULE OF NICKEL SULFATE
      WITH SEVEN MOLECULES OF WATER CHEMICALY BONDED TO EACH OTHER.
 
   3) ALL PLUSES, '+', IN THE FORMULA COLUM, MEANS TWO DIFFERENT COMPOUNDS 
      TOGETHER IN A SOLUTION.
      EXAMPLE... AQUA REGIA... HNO3 + 3HCL... IS NITRIC ACID AND HYDROCHLORIC 
      ACID MIXED TO GETHER, BUT 'NOT' CHEMICALY COMBINED.
 
   4) THE ROW A PERIODS, '..........', ALL ALONE IN A COLUM, MEANS THAT THERE 
      IS NO INFORMATION ON IT, OR YOU SHOULD CHECK UNDER THE THE OTHER NAME(S).
